Professional & Research Experience

Work at Capgemini Engineering

Since August 2022, I have been working at Capgemini Engineering as a Technical Project Leader. With a background in AI research, I sought opportunities to broaden my expertise and apply it to other cutting-edge fields. This led me to take on a project focused on developing AI solutions for autonomous vehicles. In this role, I have contributed to the development of AI-based perception modules and worked on the auto-certification of autopilots using NLP techniques. Researcher at Cerema & Université Gustave Eiffel

As Head of Research (Chargé de Recherche) at Université Gustave Eiffel, I contributed to the ACIMP project, a four-year initiative funded by the French National Research Agency, from November 2019 to July 2021. During my two-year tenure, I focused on developing advanced signal processing and AI techniques aimed at enhancing fault detection and radar signal processing for civil engineering applications. Throughout this period, I published over eight articles in leading journals and conferences. Additionally, I am proud to present my registered software, gprDetector, which has been officially registered with Agence pour la Protection des Programmes (ID n°: 130026123).
Prior to this, from November 2016 to October 2019, I was a doctoral candidate funded by Cerema (a branch of the French Ministry of Ecology & Environment) and IFSTTAR (now known as Université Gustave Eiffel). My thesis focused on developing machine learning-based methods for damage detection and monitoring through Non-Destructive Testing (NDT). Using Ground Penetrating Radars (GPRs) for data acquisition, I applied advanced signal processing and machine learning techniques to create automated damage detection and monitoring systems.
